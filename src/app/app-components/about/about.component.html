Tools for Improving Your Writing

Writer's Sauce is a web application for organizing your writing and the writing process. While it is designed for fiction writing, it can be used for any kind of writing that incorporates people, locations, things and events.

Stories

Stories are the intersection of people or characters doing something at some place. The fundamental unit of a story is a scene. All scenes take place somewhere, have a specific literary purpose and the characters in the scene have goals that reflect their state of mind in relation to the plot. Coordinating the plot and scenes is the craft of writing. Using some sauce to keep these organized let's you focus on writing.

Habitual Domains

Humans are creatures that live in states that define the patterns of what they do. A collection of these states defines a character's habitual domain. Some of these states can recur over and over while other can only happen once before shifting to another state. When a person is overwhelmed they can be thrown out of their habitual domain, struggle in unfamiliar territory until they find a new habitual domain.

Lead Characters

All novel length stories require dynamic characters that are written in a way that brings them to life for the reader. Filling out forms that describe your characters may seem fulilling, but the real value in character description comes when you ask why. Why does your lead dress a certain way, wake up at certain time or love/hate groups that protest. Having a detailed background on your main character lightens the load when writing serials or books that share characters.
